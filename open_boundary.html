Open Boundary Problems¶

Multiple effective-field terms, namely the demagnetization field (DemagField) and the Oersted field (OerstedField) involve the solution of so called open-boundary problems of the form

\[\begin{split}\begin{align} \Delta u &= f(\vec{x}) &\text{in}&\quad \omega \\ \Delta u &= 0 &\text{in}&\quad \mathbb{R}^3 \setminus \omega \\ \partial_n u &= g(\vec{x}) &\forall &\quad \vec{x} \in \partial \omega \\ u(\vec{x}) &= \mathcal{O}(1/|\vec{x}|) &\text{if}&\quad |\vec{x}| \rightarrow \infty \end{align}\end{split}\]

different algorithms are available for the solution of these problems.

Hybrid FEM-BEM Method (Fredkin and Koehler)¶

A hybrid FEM-BEM approach for the solution of open-boundary problems was propsed by Fredkin and Koehler [1]. Consider the following splitting of the solution \(u\):

\[u = u_1 + u_2\]

\(u_1\) is zero within \(\omega\) and solves the Neumann problem

\[\begin{split}\begin{align} \Delta u_1 &= f(\vec{x}) \\ \frac{\partial u_1}{\partial \vec{n}} &= g(\vec{x}) \text{ on } \partial \omega \end{align}\end{split}\]

within \(\omega\). \(u_2\) is required to solve the Laplace equation while fixing the continuity condition on \(u\). This condition is met by a double-layer potential

\[\begin{align} u_2 = \int_{\partial \omega} u_1 \frac{\partial}{\partial \vec{n}} \frac{1}{|\vec{x} - \vec{x}'|} \dx \end{align}\]
